Cybersecurity Basics

Clear, practical advice for people with any level of experience.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a trick where attackers pretend to be trusted organizations or people to steal personal information or money. Phishing messages often create a sense of urgency.

  • Emails asking to "verify account" via a link
  • Text messages promising rewards
  • Calls pretending to be support

Common Red Flags

  • Sender email looks odd (extra letters or wrong domain)
  • Urgent language: "Act now or we close your account"
  • Requests for money, passwords, or codes
  • Attachments or links you weren't expecting

Practical Steps

  1. Stop — don't click links if unsure
  2. Verify sender via phone or official site
  3. Use strong passwords and unique ones per site
  4. Turn on Two-Factor Authentication
  5. Keep your device software updated
